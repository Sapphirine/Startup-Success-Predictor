No of Companies by Funding Rounds and Status

This table shows both the values and the percentages split for the number of funding rounds (atmost 15) that companies in each status were able to reach. Some noticeable insights are:

  • 84% of companies did not go through any funding rounds.
  • After 2 fundings rounds, the number of companies that IPOed is greater than the number of closed (failed) companies.
  • We can also observe that as a startup goes through more funding rounds, the likelihood that it will IPO also increases.

Companies by Funding Round

This bar graph shows the total number of startups at each funding stage. As we can notice, the number of companies reaching the next round drops substantially with each subsequent funding round.

Companies by Funding Rounds and Status

This stacked bar chart shows the breakdown of the status for all the companies at a particular funding stage. As we can observe from the graph:

  • Private-equity has the lowest amount of companies that close and the largest amount of companies that reach IPO
  • Whereas Series-C+ has the largest number of companies that get acquired.

Feature Importance

This plot shows the top 10 most important features used by our XGBoost model in decreasing order of importance. As we can see:

  • The total amount of funding received is the most important feature for this model.
  • Number of relationships is the second most important feature.
  • Some other important features include the number of milestones, age of the startup, and the number of funding rounds that it goes through.
